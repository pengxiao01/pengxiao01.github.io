About Me

Welcome to my academic website! I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Chemical and Biological Engineering Department at Northwestern University. My research focuses on life cycle assessment, techno-economic analysis, and wastewater resource recovery.

I completed my Ph.D. in Civil Engineering from McMaster University, Canada in 2023, where I worked on wastewater simulation and uncertainty analysis. I also hold a Master of Applied Science in Civil Engineering from McMaster University (2019) and dual Bachelor's degrees in Civil Engineering and Project Management from Jilin University, China (2017).

My current research interests include:

  • Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) and Environmental Impact Analysis
  • Techno-Economic Analysis of Water Treatment Systems
  • Wastewater Resource Recovery and Sustainable Water Management
  • Machine Learning Applications in Environmental Engineering
  • Uncertainty Analysis in Environmental Systems Modeling

I am passionate about developing sustainable solutions for water and wastewater treatment, with particular expertise in environmental systems modeling and data-driven approaches. I welcome opportunities for collaboration and am interested in mentoring students in environmental engineering research.

Research

Greenhouse Gas Emissions from U.S. Wastewater Treatment

Funding: Northwestern University Postdoctoral Fellowship

Leading research to benchmark greenhouse gas emissions from U.S. wastewater treatment plants and evaluate energy usage and GHG impacts of global wastewater treatment expansion and nutrient recovery. This work contributes to sustainable water management strategies and climate impact mitigation.

Status: Manuscript under major revision at Nature Water

Life Cycle Assessment of Water Resource Recovery Systems

Funding: Northwestern University, Collaborative Research

Conducting comprehensive LCA and techno-economic analysis for novel wastewater resource recovery systems. This interdisciplinary project involves developing intelligent water treatment technologies with focus on sustainability and economic viability.

Key Publications: Multiple manuscripts in preparation, presentations at ISSST 2025

Machine Learning for Environmental Systems Modeling

Funding: Environment and Climate Change Canada, $200,000 CAD

Developed hybrid models combining process-based and data-driven approaches for complex water systems, including prediction of contaminant removal and wastewater flow forecasting. Led successful collaboration with government agencies and industry partners.

Key Publications: Front Cover Story in Frontiers of Environmental Science & Engineering, Editor's Choice in Sustainability
