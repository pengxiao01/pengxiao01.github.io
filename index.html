About Me

Welcome to my academic website! I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Chemical and Biological Engineering Department at Northwestern University, working with Dr. Jennifer B. Dunn. My research focuses on sustainable water and wastewater management, life cycle assessment, and climate impact mitigation.

I completed my Ph.D. in Civil Engineering from McMaster University, Canada in 2023, where I developed data-driven approaches for wastewater modeling under the supervision of Dr. Zoe Li. My doctoral work focused on machine learning applications, uncertainty analysis, and hybrid modeling approaches for complex environmental systems.

Research Interests:

  • Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) and Techno-Economic Analysis (TEA)
  • Greenhouse Gas Mitigation in Water, Wastewater, and Agricultural Systems
  • Machine Learning for Environmental Prediction and Modeling
  • Uncertainty and Sensitivity Analysis in Environmental Models
  • Climate-Energy-Water System Interactions
  • Consequential LCA of Large-Scale Infrastructure Transitions

My current research at Northwestern University includes benchmarking greenhouse gas emissions from U.S. wastewater treatment plants, evaluating the environmental impacts of cotton production, and developing life cycle assessments for innovative water resource recovery systems. I am passionate about contributing to evidence-based policy making and developing sustainable solutions for environmental challenges.

I welcome collaborations and am actively engaged in mentoring undergraduate and graduate students in environmental engineering research.
